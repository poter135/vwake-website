How it works · Listing Process

4 steps from sign-up to revenue.

From the moment you sign up to earning through morning letters long after you go live, you do it all yourself in the console.

Sign up & create your profile
Register with your email and fill in your creator profile (stage name, channel) in 3 minutes. No waiting for a reply — start right away.
Record & upload
Record 3 clips × 10–15 seconds each (gentle / energetic / tsundere) at home and upload them right in the console. We provide mood guidance, not a script.
Approved → go live & earn
We review your audio and identity; once approved, your voice goes live for sale → starts selling → monthly payouts on the 30th.
Keep connecting, keep earning
Going live isn't the finish line — fans pay to send you morning letters, and every text or voice reply is income. Voice packs sell passively while morning letters add an active layer, growing the bond and the revenue together.
FAQ · Frequently Asked Questions

What you'll want to know before listing

Will my voice be used to train AI?

No. Without your written consent, Vwake will never train AI voice models on your recordings, never synthesize content you didn't personally record, and never extend usage beyond the contract scope. Your audio is only downloaded and played within the Vwake app per the contract terms. If Vwake develops new AI-related features in the future, we will obtain your consent and renegotiate revenue terms — your prior authorization will not silently extend.

How does revenue share work? When do I get paid?

Revenue share model — every fan purchase earns you a cut. Specific revenue split, pricing, and payout details (bank transfer / tax handling) are shared with the partnership contract. Monthly payouts.

Do I have to reply to every morning letter? Won't it become a burden?

No — you're fully in control. Morning letters can be turned on or off anytime, and you set your own daily cap on incoming letters — lower the cap to reply less, or switch it off entirely to take a break. You only need to reply within 48 hours; if you don't, the fan is automatically refunded, so nothing piles up. You can also block specific users. This is extra income, not an obligation, and it has no effect on your voice pack sales.

Is this exclusive? Can I still sell on Booth?

Not exclusive. The traditional static voice packs you sell on Booth or Fanbox are different from Vwake's in-app alarm voice format. Vwake doesn't restrict you from continuing to sell traditional packs elsewhere — we only require app-internal usage rights for the 3 clips you record for us.

How many clips do I need to record? What's the format?

3 clips (3 moods × 1 each) performs best with fans. If recording conditions are limited, 1–2 clips is also fine — pricing and revenue share don't change.

Each clip is 10–15 seconds, recommended moods are gentle / energetic / tsundere, with wake-up greeting or "five more minutes" themes (content is freeform). The recording bar is low — your phone's built-in mic in a quiet room is enough. No professional studio needed.

My subscriber count is small. Can I still list?

Absolutely welcome! Vwake's mission has always been to bring together creators of all kinds. If you're interested in collaborating on voice content, that's wonderful news for us. Go ahead and sign up to list your voice.
